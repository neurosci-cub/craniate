Audio Illusion

McGurk Effect

This illusion was made by mixing sound and video cues. The video shows a person saying the sound “bah, ”but the audio is of someone saying the sound “gah.” Most people can identify this correctly in the activity. However, when you mix the mismatched sound and visuals, most people perceive a new sound: “Fah” (or “Dah” or “Thah”), which is a blend of the two sounds.

Watch Video

Instructions:

  • First play on mute.
  • Second play, close your eyes and listen.
  • Finally, third play with sound on and eyes open.
  • Note what sounds you sense

The Science Behind It:

This occurs because humans use sight and sound together to process speech.

In the Wild: Bad Lip Reading


If sound and visual information is not quite synched but close in timing, our brains will tweak and adjust, creating a perception of seamless, synched sound and visuals. This adjusting of inputs is also what makes bad lip reading work!
